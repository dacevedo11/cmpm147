Experiment 4A - First Glitch Project

Translated from floating-isles.glitch.me

Description

The world extends indefinitely. The tiles are like their own little floating islands, and they're all just floating through space. As you move further away from the center, the vertical range of the tiles increases, causing more extreme movements. The center is the most stable.Changing the key results in a change in the layout. The tiles move up and down over time. Just like in space, everything is consantly in movement, constantly in ortbit.

Technical

I created two variables - one that controls noise and another controls speed. These two variables are used in the drawTile function to create the movement of the tiles. I also calculated the distance from the center and created a range for x and y offset values. The noise value increases as you move away from the center, causing the tiles to move more and more. The tiles are drawn in a grid pattern, with the center tile being the most stable. The tiles move up and down over time.
